# Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the Solar System — a gas giant more than twice as massive as all other planets combined.

## Quick Facts

| Property                  | Value                      |
| ------------------------- | -------------------------- |
| Order from Sun            | 5th                        |
| Diameter                  | 139,820 km                 |
| Mass                      | 1.90 × 10²⁷ kg (318 Earth) |
| Average distance from Sun | 778.5 million km (5.20 AU) |
| Orbital period            | 11.86 Earth years          |
| Rotation period           | 9.93 hours                 |
| Moons                     | 95+ confirmed              |
| Cloud-top temperature     | ~−145 °C                   |

## Overview

Jupiter is composed mostly of hydrogen and helium, similar to the Sun. It has no solid surface — its atmosphere gradually transitions into a liquid metallic hydrogen interior. Jupiter rotates faster than any other planet, completing a day in under 10 hours.

## Notable Features

- **The Great Red Spot** — a giant storm larger than Earth that has raged for at least 350 years.
- **The Galilean moons** — Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto, discovered by Galileo in 1610.
- **A faint ring system** and the strongest magnetic field of any planet.

## Exploration

- **Pioneer** and **Voyager** probes flew by in the 1970s.
- **Galileo** orbited from 1995 to 2003.
- **Juno** has been studying Jupiter's interior and poles since 2016.
