## Overview

Mercury is a rocky, terrestrial planet with a heavily cratered surface that resembles Earth's Moon. Because it has almost no atmosphere to retain heat, it experiences the most extreme temperature swings of any planet in the Solar System.

## Notable Features

- **Caloris Basin** — one of the largest impact craters in the Solar System, about 1,550 km in diameter.
- **Enormous iron core** — Mercury's core makes up roughly 85% of its radius, giving it a higher density than expected for its size.
- **A weak magnetic field** , about 1% the strength of Earth's.

## Exploration

- **Mariner 10** (1974–1975) was the first spacecraft to visit Mercury.
- **MESSENGER** (2011–2015) orbited the planet and mapped its surface in detail.
- **BepiColombo** (launched 2018) is a joint ESA–JAXA mission en route to Mercury orbit.
