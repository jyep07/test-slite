# James Webb Space Telescope

The James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) is the largest and most powerful space telescope ever built, launched on December 25, 2021.

## Quick Facts

| Property           | Value                                                       |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| Launch date        | December 25, 2021                                           |
| Operator           | NASA / ESA / CSA                                            |
| Primary mirror     | 6.5 m (18 gold-coated segments)                             |
| Orbit              | Sun–Earth Lagrange point 2 (L2), ~1.5 million km from Earth |
| Primary wavelength | Infrared                                                    |

## Overview

Unlike the Hubble Space Telescope, which observes mainly in visible light, JWST observes in infrared. This allows it to see through cosmic dust and to detect the faint, redshifted light from the most distant and earliest galaxies in the universe. A five-layer sunshield the size of a tennis court keeps its instruments extremely cold (around −233 °C).

## Scientific Goals

- Observe the first galaxies that formed after the Big Bang.
- Study the formation of stars and planetary systems.
- Analyze the atmospheres of exoplanets for signs of habitability.
- Investigate objects within our own Solar System.

## Early Results

JWST has captured stunning images of nebulae, distant galaxies, and exoplanet atmospheres, including the detection of carbon dioxide and other molecules in worlds orbiting other stars — ushering in a new era of astronomy.
