# The Voyager Program

The Voyager program consists of two robotic probes, Voyager 1 and Voyager 2, launched by NASA in 1977 to study the outer Solar System and beyond.

## Quick Facts

| -------------------------- | ----------------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| Launch date                | Sep 5, 1977                   | Aug 20, 1977                  |
| Status                     | Active, in interstellar space | Active, in interstellar space |
| Entered interstellar space | 2012                          | 2018                          |

## Overview

The Voyagers took advantage of a rare planetary alignment that occurs once every 175 years, allowing them to visit multiple outer planets using gravitational slingshots. Voyager 2 remains the only spacecraft to have visited all four giant planets: Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune.

## Major Discoveries

- Active volcanoes on Jupiter's moon Io.
- Detailed structure of Saturn's rings.
- The first close-up views of Uranus and Neptune.
- Hints of a subsurface ocean on Europa.

## The Golden Record

Each probe carries a gold-plated copper disk containing sounds and images selected to portray the diversity of life and culture on Earth — a message for any intelligent life that might find it. The Voyagers are now the most distant human-made objects in existence.
