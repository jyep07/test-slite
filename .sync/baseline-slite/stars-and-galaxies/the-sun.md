# The Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of the Solar System and by far its most massive object, containing 99.86% of the system's total mass.

## Quick Facts

| Property            | Value                                 |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Type                | G2V main-sequence star (yellow dwarf) |
| Diameter            | 1,391,000 km (109 Earth)              |
| Mass                | 1.99 × 10³⁰ kg (333,000 Earth)        |
| Surface temperature | ~5,500 °C                             |
| Core temperature    | ~15 million °C                        |
| Age                 | ~4.6 billion years                    |
| Composition         | ~73% hydrogen, ~25% helium            |

## Overview

The Sun generates energy through nuclear fusion, converting about 600 million tons of hydrogen into helium every second. This energy radiates outward as light and heat, sustaining nearly all life on Earth. Light from the Sun takes about 8 minutes and 20 seconds to reach Earth.

## Structure

- **Core** — where fusion occurs.
- **Radiative and convective zones** — transport energy outward.
- **Photosphere** — the visible surface.
- **Chromosphere and corona** — the outer atmosphere, visible during total solar eclipses.

## Solar Activity

The Sun follows an 11-year cycle of magnetic activity, producing sunspots, solar flares, and coronal mass ejections that drive space weather and auroras. In about 5 billion years, the Sun will expand into a red giant and eventually become a white dwarf.
