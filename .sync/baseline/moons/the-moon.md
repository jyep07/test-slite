# The Moon

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite and the fifth largest moon in the
Solar System.

## Some Quick Facts

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Parent planet | Earth |
| Diameter | 3,474 km |
| Mass | 7.35 × 10²² kg (0.0123 Earth) |
| Average distance from Earth | 384,400 km |
| Orbital period | 27.3 days |
| Surface gravity | 1.62 m/s² (1/6 of Earth) |

## Overview

The Moon formed about 4.5 billion years ago, likely from debris left after a
Mars-sized body collided with the early Earth. It is tidally locked. The Moon's gravity drives ocean tides and helps
stabilize Earth's axial tilt.

## Notable Features

- **Maria** — dark basaltic plains formed by ancient volcanic eruptions, once
  mistaken for seas.
- **The far side** — heavily cratered and never visible from Earth, first
  photographed by the Soviet Luna 3 in 1959.
- **Regolith** — a layer of fine dust and broken rock covering the surface.

## Exploration

The Moon is the only celestial body beyond Earth that humans have visited.
**Apollo 11** landed the first humans in 1969, and the **Artemis program** aims
to return astronauts, including the first woman, to the lunar surface.
