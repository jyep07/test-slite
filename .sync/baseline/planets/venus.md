# Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and the hottest planet in the Solar
System, often called Earth's "sister planet" due to their similar size.

## Quick Facts

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Order from Sun | 2nd |
| Diameter | 12,104 km |
| Mass | 4.87 × 10²⁴ kg (0.815 Earth) |
| Average distance from Sun | 108.2 million km (0.72 AU) |
| Orbital period | 225 Earth days |
| Rotation period | 243 Earth days (retrograde) |
| Moons | 0 |
| Surface temperature | ~465 °C |

## Overview

Venus is shrouded in a thick atmosphere of carbon dioxide with clouds of
sulfuric acid. A runaway greenhouse effect makes its surface hotter than
Mercury's, despite being farther from the Sun. The surface pressure is about
92 times that of Earth — equivalent to being 900 m underwater.

## Notable Features

- **Retrograde rotation** — Venus spins in the opposite direction to most
  planets, so the Sun rises in the west and sets in the east.
- **A day longer than its year** — one rotation takes longer than one orbit.
- **Volcanic plains and highland regions** such as Ishtar Terra and Aphrodite
  Terra.

## Exploration

- **Venera program** (USSR) achieved the first soft landing on another planet
  in 1970.
- **Magellan** (1990–1994) mapped 98% of the surface using radar.
- Upcoming missions: NASA's **DAVINCI** and **VERITAS**, and ESA's **EnVision**.
