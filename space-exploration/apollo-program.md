# The Apollo Program

The Apollo program was NASA's effort to land humans on the Moon and bring them
safely back to Earth, carried out between 1961 and 1972.

## Overview

Initiated in response to President Kennedy's 1961 goal of landing a man on the
Moon before the end of the decade, Apollo became one of the greatest
achievements in human history. It used the massive **Saturn V** rocket, still
the most powerful rocket ever flown to operational service.

## Key Missions

| Mission | Year | Significance |
|---------|------|--------------|
| Apollo 1 | 1967 | Cabin fire killed three astronauts during a test |
| Apollo 8 | 1968 | First crewed mission to orbit the Moon |
| Apollo 11 | 1969 | First crewed Moon landing |
| Apollo 13 | 1970 | "Successful failure" — crew survived an explosion |
| Apollo 17 | 1972 | Last crewed mission to the Moon |

## Apollo 11

On July 20, 1969, Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first humans to
walk on the Moon, while Michael Collins orbited above. Armstrong's words — "That's
one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind" — were broadcast to an
estimated 650 million people.

## Legacy

Twelve astronauts walked on the Moon across six successful landings, returning
382 kg of lunar samples that continue to inform our understanding of the Moon's
origin and the early Solar System.
