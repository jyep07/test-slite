# The International Space Station

The International Space Station (ISS) is a large, habitable spacecraft in low
Earth orbit — a collaborative project among five space agencies.

## Quick Facts

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Partners | NASA, Roscosmos, ESA, JAXA, CSA |
| First module launched | 1998 (Zarya) |
| Continuously crewed since | November 2000 |
| Orbital altitude | ~400 km |
| Orbital speed | ~28,000 km/h |
| Time per orbit | ~90 minutes |
| Mass | ~420,000 kg |

## Overview

The ISS serves as a microgravity laboratory where crews conduct experiments in
biology, physics, astronomy, and materials science that are impossible on Earth.
It orbits the planet about 16 times per day, meaning astronauts see roughly 16
sunrises and sunsets daily.

## Life Aboard

- Crews typically stay for about six months.
- Everything from sleeping to eating must account for weightlessness.
- Water is recycled, including moisture from the air and urine.
- Regular exercise (about 2 hours/day) counteracts muscle and bone loss.

## Fun Facts

- The ISS is the largest human-made object ever placed in orbit.

## Legacy and Future

Continuously occupied for over two decades, the ISS represents the longest
sustained human presence in space. It is expected to be retired around 2030,
with commercial space stations planned to succeed it.
