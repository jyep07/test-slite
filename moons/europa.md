# Europa

Europa is the smallest of Jupiter’s 4 Galilean moons and one of the most promising places to search for extraterrestrial life.

## Quick Facts

| Property                      | Value               |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------- |
| Parent planet                 | Jupiter             |
| Diameter                      | 3,122 km            |
| Average distance from Jupiter | 671,000 km          |
| Orbital period                | 3.55 days           |
| Surface temperature           | ~−160 °C to −220 °C |

## Overview

Europa has a smooth, icy surface crisscrossed by reddish-brown cracks. Beneath

its ice shell lies a global saltwater ocean — estimated to contain twice as much

water as all of Earth’s oceans combined. Tidal heating from Jupiter keeps this

ocean liquid.

## Why It Matters

The combination of liquid water, chemical energy, and the right elements makes

Europa a key target in astrobiology. If life exists, it may thrive near

hydrothermal vents on the ocean floor, similar to extremophiles on Earth.

## Exploration

- **NASA’s Europa Clipper** (launched 2024) will conduct dozens of close flybys to assess the moon’s habitability.
- **ESA’s JUICE** mission will also study Europa along with Ganymede and Callisto.
