# Titan

Titan is Saturn's largest moon and the second largest in the Solar System —
the only moon with a substantial atmosphere.

## Quick Facts

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Parent planet | Saturn |
| Diameter | 5,150 km |
| Average distance from Saturn | 1.22 million km |
| Orbital period | 15.9 days |
| Surface temperature | ~−179 °C |

## Overview

Titan is larger than the planet Mercury and is the only known world besides
Earth with stable liquid on its surface. Its dense, nitrogen-rich atmosphere is
hazy with orange hydrocarbons. Instead of water, Titan has lakes and rivers of
liquid methane and ethane.

## Notable Features

- **Methane cycle** — Titan has a "hydrological" cycle of methane rain, rivers,
  lakes, and clouds, analogous to Earth's water cycle.
- **Kraken Mare** — the largest known liquid methane sea, comparable in size to
  the Caspian Sea.
- A possible subsurface water ocean beneath the icy crust.

## Exploration

The **Huygens probe** landed on Titan in 2005 — the most distant landing ever
achieved. NASA's **Dragonfly** rotorcraft mission, planned for the 2030s, will
fly across Titan's surface to study its chemistry and habitability.
