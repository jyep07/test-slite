## Overview

Saturn is a gas giant composed mainly of hydrogen and helium. It is the least dense planet in the Solar System — so light that it would float in water if a large enough ocean existed. Its low density gives it a noticeably flattened, oblate shape.

## Notable Features

- **The rings** — made of billions of ice and rock particles ranging from dust grains to house-sized chunks, spanning up to 282,000 km wide but only about 10 m thick on average.
- **Titan** — Saturn's largest moon, larger than Mercury, with a thick atmosphere and lakes of liquid methane.
- **Enceladus** — an icy moon that erupts water-vapor geysers from a subsurface ocean.

## Exploration

The **Cassini–Huygens** mission (2004–2017) revolutionized our understanding of Saturn, its rings, and moons, and landed the Huygens probe on Titan.
