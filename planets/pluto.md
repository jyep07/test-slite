# Pluto

A dwarf planet in the Kuiper Belt, and from 1930 to 2006 counted as the
ninth planet from the Sun before being reclassified.

| Average distance from Sun | 5.9 billion km (39.5 AU) |

## Overview

Pluto is a small, icy world on the frozen outer edge of the Solar System. Its
orbit is highly elliptical and tilted, sometimes bringing it closer to the Sun
than Neptune. It was reclassified from planet to dwarf planet in 2006 after the
International Astronomical Union adopted a formal definition of "planet" that
Pluto does not meet, because it has not cleared its orbital neighborhood.

## Notable Features

- **Tombaugh Regio** — a large, bright, heart-shaped plain of nitrogen ice.
- **Charon** — Pluto's largest moon, roughly half Pluto's diameter, so large
  that the two bodies orbit a shared point in space between them.
- A thin nitrogen atmosphere that expands and freezes as Pluto moves nearer to
  and farther from the Sun.

## Exploration

Pluto was visited for the first and only time by NASA's **New Horizons**
spacecraft, which flew past in July 2015 and returned the first detailed images
of its surface.
