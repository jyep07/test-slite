# Planets

An introduction to the planets of the Solar System, and a guide to the profiles
in this folder.

## What is a planet?

The International Astronomical Union defines a planet as a body that (1) orbits
the Sun, (2) has enough mass for its own gravity to pull it into a roughly round
shape, and (3) has cleared its orbital neighborhood of other debris. Eight
bodies meet all three criteria. Objects that satisfy the first two but not the
third — like Pluto — are classified as **dwarf planets**.

## The eight planets

Ordered by distance from the Sun:

| # | Planet | Type |
|---|--------|------|
| 1 | Mercury | Terrestrial |
| 2 | Venus | Terrestrial |
| 3 | Earth | Terrestrial |
| 4 | Mars | Terrestrial |
| 5 | Jupiter | Gas giant |
| 6 | Saturn | Gas giant |
| 7 | Uranus | Ice giant |
| 8 | Neptune | Ice giant |

The four inner planets are small, rocky **terrestrial** worlds. The four outer
planets are far larger: **gas giants** (Jupiter, Saturn) composed mostly of
hydrogen and helium, and **ice giants** (Uranus, Neptune) rich in heavier
volatiles like water, ammonia, and methane.
