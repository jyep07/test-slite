# The Milky Way

The Milky Way is the barred spiral galaxy that contains our Solar System.

## Quick Facts

| Property           | Value                                     |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------------- |
| Type               | Barred spiral galaxy                      |
| Diameter           | ~100,000–105,000 light-years              |
| Number of stars    | 100–400 billion                           |
| Age                | ~13.6 billion years                       |
| Central black hole | Sagittarius A\* (~4 million solar masses) |

## Overview

The Milky Way is one of hundreds of billions of galaxies in the observable universe. It contains hundreds of billions of stars. Our Solar System sits in one of its spiral arms — the Orion Arm — about 26,000 light-years from the galactic center. The Sun takes roughly 225–250 million years to complete one orbit of the galaxy (a "galactic year").

## Structure

- **Galactic bulge** — a dense central region of older stars.
- **Spiral arms** — regions of active star formation, including the Orion, Perseus, and Sagittarius arms.
- **Galactic halo** — a spherical region of old stars, globular clusters, and dark matter surrounding the disk.

## Neighbors

The Milky Way is part of the **Local Group**, a cluster of more than 50 galaxies. Its largest neighbor, the **Andromeda Galaxy**, is on a collision course with the Milky Way and is expected to merge with it in about 4.5 billion years.
